# Win Story Template (Amazon Working Backwards)

> Write this BEFORE demo prep research. It forces clarity on what success looks like.

## Template

```
WIN STORY: [Company] — [Person Name]
[Person] signs [$X/mo] [plan]. [Company]'s [current pain] transforms to [future state].
[Specific metric] improves [X%] within [timeframe] because [why Sprites makes the difference].
[Person] becomes internal champion — refers [X] similar companies within [timeframe].
Deciding factor: [the one moment in the demo/process that tipped the decision].
Almost killed it: [the objection that nearly derailed — and how it was overcome].
```

## Example

```
WIN STORY: Curion Insights — Stephanie Zielinski
Stephanie signs $500/mo Build plan. Curion's content team stops doing paid media manually.
Campaign performance improves 40% within 60 days because AI handles optimization 24/7.
Stephanie becomes internal champion — recommends Sprites to 2 sister agencies.
Deciding factor: saw the time-savings quantified (260 hrs/year) during demo.
Almost killed it: "We already have an agency" — countered with cost comparison + 24/7 advantage.
```

## How It's Used

1. Write Win Story BEFORE any research (forces you to think about the end state)
2. Research fills in the gaps (verify assumptions, find the real numbers)
3. Demo flow designed to hit the "deciding factor" moment
4. Cheat sheet organized around making the Win Story happen
5. After deal closes: compare Win Story to actual outcome

## Post-Close Comparison

After every deal close (won or lost), compare:
- Did the deciding factor match? Y/N
- Did the predicted objection come up? Y/N
- Was the outcome close to predicted? Y/N
- What was different? [notes]

Track accuracy over time. As win story predictions get more accurate, demo prep gets more targeted.

## Integration with Loop

- Win Story accuracy feeds PATTERNS.md under ## Win Story Accuracy
- Predicted deciding factors that match actual → strengthen that demo approach
- Predicted objections that come up → validate objection prep is working
- Over time: "For solo founders, the deciding factor is always time-savings, not ROI" — pattern emerges
