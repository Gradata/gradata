# Outbound Sales Playbook

Outbound is a numbers game with skill multipliers. Volume matters, but skill at each step compounds results. This skill provides the tactical framework for building and running multi-channel outbound campaigns for Sprites.ai.

## The Foundation: Your List

The quality of the list determines the ceiling of the campaign. Great copy on a bad list produces nothing. Mediocre copy on a perfect list still books meetings.

**List criteria for Sprites:**
- Company size: 10-300 employees
- Running Meta Pixel and/or Google Ads
- Titles: VP/Director/Head of Marketing, CMO, Founder, Performance Marketing Manager, Agency Principal
- Geography: US primary; UK/CA/AU/NZ/Western Europe secondary
- Signals: recently changed agencies, new website, scaling ad spend, hiring for marketing roles, commented on relevant LinkedIn posts

**List hygiene (do before every campaign):**
- Verify emails — bounce rate above 3% destroys deliverability
- Remove duplicates across active sequences
- Flag competitors and existing customers
- Update titles quarterly — people change jobs fast

## The Cadence Structure

See [references/cadence-templates.md](references/cadence-templates.md) for the full 12-touch, 3-week cadence with email templates for each step.

**The principle:** Multi-channel always beats single-channel. Phone + email + social together outperform any one alone. Each touch adds new information or a new angle — never "just following up."

**High-level structure:**
- **Week 1 (high intensity):** Email 1 + LinkedIn connect (Day 1), Phone + VM (Day 2), Email 2 with case study (Day 3), Phone (Day 5)
- **Week 2 (multi-angle):** Email 3 value-add (Day 7), Phone (Day 8), LinkedIn message (Day 10), Email 4 social proof (Day 12)
- **Week 3 (close the loop):** Phone + VM (Day 14), Email 5 breakup (Day 17), Final LinkedIn (Day 19), Last call (Day 21)

**Rules:**
- Never send more than 1 email/day to the same person
- Vary send times
- If they engage (open, click, reply), accelerate
- If they say no, stop immediately

## Cold Call Script

**Opening (first 10 seconds):**
"Hi [Name], this is Oliver from Sprites. How have you been?"

The "how have you been" pattern interrupt implies familiarity. They'll usually say "good, who is this?" — which is an engaged response, not a brush-off.

**Bridge (next 15 seconds):**
"The reason I'm reaching out — I work with marketing teams at companies like [similar company]. They were spending [X hours/dollars] on manual campaign management, and we helped them [specific result]. I'm curious if that's something you're dealing with too?"

**Close for meeting:**
"Sounds like there might be a fit. I'd love to show you what this looks like — takes about 20 minutes. Do you have time [specific day] or [specific day]?"

Always offer two specific time slots. "When works for you?" creates decision fatigue.

## Objection Handling

**Acknowledge, redirect, bridge to value.** Never argue.

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have a tool/agency" | "Most of our clients did too. The difference was [specific]. Can I show you in 15 minutes?" |
| "We don't have budget" | "H.M. Cole actually replaced their agency spend entirely — Sprites was cheaper than one retainer. Want to see the math?" |
| "Send me info" | "Happy to. So I send the right thing — are you more focused on paid ads, SEO, or multi-brand management right now?" |
| "Not the right person" | "Appreciate that. Who handles paid media / marketing automation on your team?" |
| "We're locked into a contract" | "When does that come up for review? I'd love to get on your radar before then." |

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Emails sent/day | 50-100 |
| Calls/day | 30-50 |
| Connect rate (phone) | 5-15% |
| Reply rate (email) | 5-15% |
| Meeting set rate (from conversations) | 20-30% |
| Show rate | 80%+ |

**Diagnosing drop-off:**
- Low opens → fix subject lines
- Opens but no replies → fix body copy and CTA
- Replies but no meetings → fix reply handling
- Meetings but no shows → fix confirmation process
