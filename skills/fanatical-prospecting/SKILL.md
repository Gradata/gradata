# Fanatical Prospecting

The single biggest reason sales pipelines dry up is insufficient prospecting activity. Not bad messaging, not wrong ICP, not poor timing. Just not enough touches. This skill exists to keep Oliver's pipeline full by making prospecting a daily discipline, not something that happens when there's free time.

## Why This Matters for Sprites

Oliver owns the full sales cycle at Sprites.ai. There's no BDR team feeding him leads. If he doesn't prospect, the pipeline dies in 90 days. Every framework below is tuned for his reality: selling an AI marketing platform to multi-brand ecom operators, solo paid media consultants, and lean marketing teams.

## Three Laws to Internalize

**The 30-Day Rule:** Whatever prospecting Oliver does (or skips) today pays off 90 days from now. A slow week in March means an empty calendar in June. This lag makes it dangerously easy to coast after a good month.

**The Law of Replacement:** Every deal that closes, advances, or dies must be replaced with fresh pipeline. Winning a deal is not permission to stop prospecting — it's a signal to prospect harder while momentum is high.

**The Universal Law of Need:** Desperation kills deals. When Oliver needs a deal to hit quota, prospects smell it. A full pipeline is the only cure — it gives him the confidence to walk away from bad fits.

## The Prospecting Pyramid

Prioritize by conversion probability. Time is finite, so spend it where it converts best:

1. **Inbound leads** — site signups, paywall drop-offs. Respond within 5 minutes. Speed wins.
2. **Orphaned accounts** — existing Sprites users with no active rep contact.
3. **Referrals** — warm intros from closed-won customers (H.M. Cole, Startup Science, Iris Finance).
4. **Targeted prospects** — ICP-fit from LinkedIn post commenters (AdsGPT list, Jayant list), Apollo research.
5. **Pure cold** — Apollo/Clay lists, cold email campaigns via Instantly.

The pyramid isn't a ranking of importance — it's a time allocation guide. Start each day at the top and work down.

## Time Blocking

Prospecting happens in protected blocks, not in spare moments between meetings. The reason this matters: context-switching kills call confidence. Three calls scattered across a day feel harder than thirty calls in one focused block.

**Recommended blocks for Oliver:**
- **Morning (1-2 hours):** Phone calls and voicemails. Connect rates peak before 9am and between 4-6pm.
- **Midday (30-60 min):** Email sequences, LinkedIn engagement, research for tomorrow's calls.

During a prospecting block, do nothing else. No inbox, no Slack, no CRM admin. The block is sacred.

## Five Channels

Effective prospecting uses all five channels together. Phone and email alone leave opportunities on the table.

1. **Phone** — Highest impact per touch. Most reps avoid it, which means less noise for the prospects who do pick up.
2. **Email** — Volume play and warm-up. Sends before/after calls increase recognition.
3. **Social (LinkedIn)** — Engage with content before pitching. A like or comment today makes the cold call tomorrow feel warmer.
4. **Text** — After initial contact: "Hey [Name], just left you a VM about your ad accounts. Worth a quick chat?"
5. **In-person** — Industry events, conferences. Less frequent but high-trust.

## Cold Call Framework

See [references/call-scripts.md](references/call-scripts.md) for the full script library with Sprites-specific openers, objection turnarounds, and voicemail templates.

**The core principle:** You have 5 seconds before they decide whether to hang up. The opener must be about THEM, not about Sprites. State your name, give a reason for calling tied to their world, and ask for 30 seconds.

**Quick reference opener:**
"Hi [Name], Oliver from Sprites. I saw you're managing paid across [X brands] — we just helped a similar team cut their agency spend to zero and hit 5.8x ROAS. Worth 30 seconds?"

## Prospecting Math

Work backwards from the goal. If Oliver needs 5 demos per week:
- ~20-30% of conversations convert to meetings
- ~10-15% of dials connect to a conversation
- So: 5 demos = ~20 conversations = ~150-200 dials/week = ~30-40 dials/day

Track these three numbers daily: dials, conversations, meetings set. The numbers don't lie and they reveal exactly where the bottleneck is.

## Mental Toughness

Prospecting rejection feels personal but isn't. The prospect is rejecting the interruption, not Oliver. Understanding this distinction is what separates reps who burn out from reps who thrive.

Five principles:
1. **Never leave a block early.** If you planned 90 minutes, do 90 minutes — even if the first 30 were brutal.
2. **Celebrate activity, not just outcomes.** 50 dials with zero connects is still better than zero dials.
3. **Don't cherry-pick the list.** Your assumptions about who will convert are wrong more often than right.
4. **The phone weighs 1,000 pounds.** Everyone feels this. Pick it up anyway.
5. **Rejection is data.** Track what brush-offs you hear most and sharpen your turnarounds for those specific ones.

## How to Apply This Skill

When Oliver asks for prospecting help:
1. Start by asking about pipeline health — how many active deals, how many demos booked this week?
2. If pipeline is thin, prioritize activity over strategy. More touches beats better touches when the funnel is empty.
3. Help build call lists, draft voicemail scripts, write pre/post-call emails.
4. Hold him accountable to daily activity numbers.
5. If he says he's too busy with demos to prospect — push back. Both happen every day. Demos fill this week's calendar; prospecting fills next month's.
