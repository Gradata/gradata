# Call Scripts & Objection Turnarounds

All scripts customized for Oliver Le, AE at Sprites.ai.

## Cold Call Openers

### The 5-Second Template
"Hi [Name], this is Oliver with Sprites. I noticed [specific observation]. The reason I'm calling is [one sentence value prop tied to their pain]. Do you have 30 seconds for me to tell you why, and you can decide if it's worth a longer conversation?"

### Multi-Brand Operator
"Hi [Name], Oliver from Sprites. I saw you're running paid across [X] brands — we just helped an 8-brand menswear rollup cut every agency retainer to zero and hit 5.8x ROAS with AI automation. Worth 30 seconds to see if it's relevant?"

### Solo Consultant / Agency
"Hi [Name], Oliver from Sprites. I noticed you're managing paid media for [X] clients. Curious — are you still handling campaign optimization manually across all those accounts, or have you started automating any of it?"

### Post-Demo Follow-Up (No Show / Went Dark)
"Hi [Name], Oliver from Sprites. We connected last week and I showed you the platform. I know things get busy — just wanted to see if you had any questions or if there's a good time to pick up where we left off."

### Inbound Signup
"Hi [Name], Oliver from Sprites. I saw you signed up on the site today — just wanted to say hi and see if you had any questions about what you saw. Most people I talk to are curious about [paid ads automation / SEO / multi-brand management] — which one caught your eye?"

## Objection Turnarounds (Prospecting Brush-Offs)

These are not closing objections. These are reflexive brush-offs to end the interruption. The goal is to earn 30 more seconds, not close a deal.

### "I'm busy / not a good time"
"I figured you would be — that's exactly why I kept this short. All I need is 30 seconds to tell you why I called, and you can decide if it's worth scheduling a proper conversation. Fair?"

**Why this works:** Acknowledges their reality instead of fighting it. "Fair?" is a micro-commitment that's hard to say no to.

### "Just send me an email"
"Happy to. So I send you the right thing, can I ask one quick question? [Ask about their current setup — e.g., 'Are you mostly focused on Meta, Google, or both right now?']. Great — I'll send that over today. What's the best email?"

**Why this works:** Turns a brush-off into a mini-discovery question. You get data AND their email.

### "We're all set / not interested"
"Totally fair. Most of the teams I talk to felt the same way before they saw what AI execution actually looks like versus the tools they're already using. Can I ask — are you still managing campaigns manually across your ad accounts?"

**Why this works:** Doesn't argue. Normalizes their response ("most teams felt the same"). The question opens a crack.

### "How did you get my number?"
"I found you on LinkedIn and thought what you're doing at [Company] was worth reaching out about. I promise I'm not going to waste your time."

**Why this works:** Honest and direct. No corporate doublespeak.

### "We already use [tool/agency]"
"Makes sense — most of the teams we work with were in the same spot. The difference they found was that Sprites actually executes inside the ad accounts, not just recommends. H.M. Cole replaced three agencies entirely. Would it be worth 15 minutes to see the comparison?"

### "What does it cost?"
"Depends on your setup, but our Starter plan is $60/month — less than one hour of an agency's time. The real question is whether AI automation can replace manual work for your team. Want to see a quick demo?"

## Voicemail Scripts (15-20 seconds max)

### Standard
"Hi [Name], Oliver Le with Sprites. I'm calling because [one specific reason tied to them — e.g., 'I saw you're running paid media across multiple brands']. I'll shoot you a quick email with details. My number is [number]. Again, Oliver from Sprites."

### After LinkedIn Engagement
"Hi [Name], Oliver Le with Sprites. I saw your comment on [post topic] and thought what you said about [specific point] was spot on. Shooting you an email with something I think you'll find relevant. Talk soon."

### Follow-Up VM (2nd or 3rd attempt)
"Hi [Name], Oliver again from Sprites. Just following up from last week. I'll send a quick note with a case study that's relevant to what you're doing at [Company]. My number is [number] if you want to chat."

**Voicemail rules:**
- Never say "please call me back" — they won't
- Always follow up with an email within 5 minutes of leaving the VM
- The voicemail's job is name recognition, not a callback. When the email lands, they'll think "oh, that guy who called."
