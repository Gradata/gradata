# Overcoming Indecision & Closing
# Sources: The JOLT Effect (Dixon/McKenna), Sell the Way You Buy (David Priemer)
# Auto-load when: deal stalling, "let me think about it", follow-up strategy, close attempts

## The Real Enemy: Indecision, Not Status Quo (JOLT Effect)
Research across 2.5M sales calls found:
- 56% of deals lost to inaction are due to customer INDECISION (not status quo preference)
- Only 44% are genuine status quo bias
- Customers who WANT to buy still fail to act because of fear

## Three Fears Behind Indecision (JOLT Effect)
1. **Valuation problems** — "Am I choosing the right option?" Too many choices, can't compare.
2. **Lack of information** — "Have I done enough homework?" Fear of not being informed enough.
3. **Outcome uncertainty** — "Will I actually get what I'm paying for?" Even if they choose right, will it deliver?

## The Omission Bias
- People fear errors of commission (doing something wrong) MORE than errors of omission (failing to do something right)
- Making a bad purchase = personal, concrete, attributable to one person
- Missing an opportunity = abstract, shared blame, can kick the can
- Result: customers would rather miss out than mess up

## The JOLT Framework
- **J — Judge the indecision:** Is this real interest with fear, or genuine disinterest? Look for language like "let me think about it," "I need to run this by my team," "can you send more info?"
- **O — Offer your recommendation:** Don't give more options. Give ONE clear recommendation. Reduce decision burden. "Based on what you've told me, here's exactly what I'd do."
- **L — Limit the downside:** Address what they're afraid of. Risk reversal. Money-back. Low entry tier. "If it doesn't work, here's what happens: nothing. You cancel."
- **T — Take it off the table:** Paradoxically, giving them an easy out increases commitment. "If this isn't a fit, I'll tell you myself."

## Sell the Way You Buy (Priemer) — Key Principles

### The Unconscious Seller Problem
Most sellers use tactics they would never respond to themselves. They don't stop to think about it. Ask yourself: "Would this approach work on ME?"

### Science + Empathy + Execution
Every sales approach needs all three:
- **Science** — understand the behavioral psychology behind why tactics work or don't
- **Empathy** — actually feel what it's like to be in the customer's shoes
- **Execution** — deliver with the right tone, timing, and medium

### Never Say "Does That Make Sense?"
Subconsciously interpreted as either:
1. "I think you might be too dumb to understand this"
2. "I'm bad at explaining things"

Replace with: "What questions do you have?" or "How does that land?"

### The Reverse Psychology Tactic — Handle with Care
When a customer says "Product B is cheaper":
- BAD: "Well go buy Product B then!" (antagonistic)
- GOOD: "You're right, we cost more. We're not for everyone. What makes you lean away from Product B?" (curious, agreeable)

Same tactic, completely different tone = completely different result.

### Tone > Tactic
The same words delivered with different tone produce opposite results. Over email, stern tone is easily imagined. In person, empathetic tone can save anything. Match medium to message sensitivity.

## For Sprites — Indecision Playbook

### When they say "let me think about it":
Don't send a follow-up deck. Don't give more info (feeds the "lack of information" fear).
Instead: "Totally get it. Honestly the easiest way to know is a 20-min demo. You'll know in 5 minutes if it's relevant. If not, I'll tell you myself. How's [specific day/time]?"

### When they say "I need to run this by my team":
"Makes sense. What would be most helpful — should I join that conversation to answer technical questions, or would a one-pager be better for you to share?"

### The Low-Risk Close:
"We have a $60/month proof of concept. Connect two brand accounts, run it 30 days, see what it surfaces. If it doesn't show value worth the $500 tier, cancel. Nothing lost. Want to start there?"

### When a deal stalls:
1. Check: is this indecision or disinterest? (Did they engage deeply in demo? Ask specific questions? Show pain?)
2. If indecision: reduce options, give recommendation, limit downside
3. If disinterest: move to referral ask and move on
4. Never chase a dead deal past 3 unanswered touches
