# Cold Email Frameworks
# Sources: Cold Email Manifesto (Alex Berman), Outbound Sales No Fluff (Reisert/Bray)
# Auto-load when: drafting cold emails, writing email sequences, Instantly campaigns

## Core Rules (Cold Email Manifesto)
1. **Hyper-specific offer** — not "we do marketing" but "we manage paid ads across multiple brands for DTC companies doing $5M+"
2. **15-20 emails at a time**, highly personalized. NOT mass blast.
3. **$2K+ deal size** — below that, cold email ROI doesn't work. Use ads instead.
4. **Measure like a scientist** — open rate, reply rate, meeting rate. Iterate subject lines and body independently.
5. **Benchmark:** 4-8 meetings per 100 emails if done right.

## Email Structure (Outbound Sales No Fluff)
- **Get to the point** — every word must earn its place
- **Leverage the persona** — tailor to their specific role problems (CMO cares about strategy, Director cares about execution)
- **Single CTA** — one ask per email. Don't ask them to click, read, AND reply.
- **Don't ask for the world** — the bigger the request, the less likely compliance

## Cold Email Formula
```
Subject: [Specific to their situation, under 6 words]

[1 line acknowledging their current state — specific to their company/role]
[1 line naming the gap without pitching]
[1 line pointing to future state or social proof]
[Soft CTA — question, not demand]
```

## What Makes It NOT Spam
- Personalized to their specific situation (company, role, pain)
- Sent to the right person (someone who'd actually buy)
- Solves a real problem they have
- Low volume, high relevance
- If they say "this is spam" — ask why. Wrong contact? Not personalized enough? Fix and iterate.

## Sequence Structure (4-step)
1. **Day 1:** Problem-aware email — name their pain, soft ask
2. **Day 3:** Value email — case study or specific result, same CTA
3. **Day 7:** Breakup angle — "figured this wasn't a priority, but wanted to share [insight]"
4. **Day 14:** Final touch — new angle or referral ask

## Channel Truth (Outbound Sales No Fluff)
- No channel is "dead." All channels work with some prospects some of the time.
- No prospect sits waiting for you to contact them. Outbound is always an intrusion — make it a valuable one.
- Difficult != ineffective. If you suck at email, that doesn't mean email doesn't work.
- Start with one channel, master it, then add another.
