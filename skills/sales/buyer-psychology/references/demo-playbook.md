# Demo Playbook for Sprites

## Pre-Demo Checklist

Before every demo, answer these four questions:
1. What do I know about their **specific** pain? (From discovery, LinkedIn, Apollo research)
2. Which **case study** most mirrors their situation? (H.M. Cole for multi-brand, Startup Science for agency pain, Iris Finance for SEO/solo marketer)
3. What's the **lowest-friction next step** I can propose? (Connect one account, run an audit, start on Starter)
4. If I were them, what would make me say **yes today**?

## Demo Structure

### 1. Recap Their Pain (30 seconds)
Don't jump into the product. Remind them why they're here.

"Before I show you anything — you mentioned [X, Y, Z]. I want to make sure we focus on what matters most to you. Which of those is the biggest pain right now?"

This does two things: confirms you listened, and lets them prioritize what they care about most.

### 2. Show the Solution (10-15 minutes)
For each pain point they named, show the exact feature that solves it. Pause after each:

"Does that address what you were describing?"

Their answer tells you if you're on track. If they say "sort of" or hesitate, dig deeper before moving on.

**Feature-to-pain mapping for Sprites:**

| Pain | Show This |
|------|-----------|
| No visibility across brands | Unified dashboard, multi-account view |
| Agency not transparent | Historical campaign data pull, spend analysis |
| Manual campaign management | Autonomous campaign launch, budget shifting |
| No SEO / organic traffic | SEO audit, automated blog publishing, schema markup |
| AI-curious but execution gap | Side-by-side: what ChatGPT does vs. what Sprites does |

### 3. Case Study Bridge (2 minutes)
After showing the product, connect it to a real result.

"This is exactly what [company] was dealing with. They were [pain]. After connecting Sprites, [specific result in specific timeframe]."

### 4. Ask for Reaction (1 minute)
Don't end with "any questions?" — that invites silence. Instead:

- "How does this compare to what you were expecting?"
- "What stood out to you?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, how relevant is this to what you're dealing with?"

### 5. Propose Next Step (1 minute)
Be specific. Don't say "let me know if you're interested."

"The natural next step is to connect one of your ad accounts and see what Sprites surfaces. Takes about 10 minutes and the audit alone is worth it. Want to do that now, or should we schedule it for [specific day]?"

## Common Demo Mistakes

1. **Showing features they didn't ask about.** Every minute spent on irrelevant features is a minute their attention drops.
2. **Spending 20 minutes on setup.** Get to the value fast. Nobody cares how the sausage is made.
3. **Letting them drive with "can you show me X?"** before you've set context. Guide the demo; let them ask questions within your framework.
4. **Talking for 10 minutes straight without checking in.** Pause every 3-4 minutes. Ask a question. Get them engaged.
5. **Ending without a next step.** If you leave the demo without scheduling something, the deal is already dying.

## The "Would I Buy This?" Test

Before every demo, read your plan and ask:
- If I were a Head of Marketing getting 50 cold emails a day, would this demo make me stop and pay attention?
- If I were managing 5 brands across agencies, would this make me want to switch?
- If I just saw this demo, would the proposed next step feel easy and low-risk?

If no to any of these, adjust before the call.
