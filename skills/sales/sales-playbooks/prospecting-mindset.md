# Prospecting Mindset & Pipeline Management
# Sources: Fanatical Prospecting (Jeb Blount), Outbound Sales No Fluff (Reisert/Bray)
# Auto-load when: prospecting, lead scoring, pipeline review, campaign planning

## 7 Mindsets of Fanatical Prospectors (Blount)
1. **Optimistic and enthusiastic** — attack each day fired up. Even bad days, make one more call.
2. **Competitive** — hardwired to win. Outwork and outhustle competitors.
3. **Confident** — expect to win. Manage fear, uncertainty, doubt.
4. **Relentless** — never give up. Use rejection as fuel.
5. **Thirsty for knowledge** — consume books, podcasts, training. View setbacks as learning.
6. **Systematic and efficient** — protect golden hours, block time, concentrate power.
7. **Adaptive and flexible** — adopt new ideas, adapt them, become adept. Early adopters.

## The Laws
- **Universal Law of Need:** The more you need the deal, the worse you perform. Desperation is felt instantly. Only cure: full pipeline.
- **30-Day Rule:** Prospecting you do today fills pipeline 30 days from now. Never stop adding to top of funnel even when demos are flowing.
- **Law of Replacement:** Every deal closed must be replaced immediately with new prospects at the top.

## Balanced Prospecting (Blount)
Never put all eggs in one basket. Mix:
- Phone (highest conversion for real conversations)
- Email (warming, familiarity building)
- Social (LinkedIn, relationship cultivation)
- Referrals (warmest leads)
- Inbound (always work until disqualified)

The right mix depends on your territory, product, and tenure. New reps need more cold calling. Tenured reps leverage relationships.

## Lead Bucketing System (Outbound Sales No Fluff)
Work buckets in REVERSE order (highest value first):

1. **Bucket 4 — Scheduled:** Confirm appointments. Monitor like a hawk. Call 2-3 hrs before if no email confirm.
2. **Bucket 3 — Priority:** Spoke but couldn't schedule. Heavy email engagers. Inbound in swimlane. Failed-to-close from 6-9 months ago.
3. **Bucket 2 — Working:** At least one verified dial. Maintain 50-100 leads minimum. Call regularly, continue multi-channel.
4. **Bucket 1 — Uncontacted:** New leads. Remove out-of-swimlane. Research, then attempt contact.

## Swimlane Definition (Outbound Sales No Fluff)
Define your ICP by:
- **Size** (employees, revenue, locations)
- **Geography** (territory boundaries)
- **Industry** (be specific — "DTC ecommerce" not "companies")
- **Related Technologies** (uses Meta Ads, Google Ads, Shopify)
- **Budget** (can afford $500+/mo)
- **News/Triggers** (hiring marketers, launching new brands, raising funding)

WARNING: The more desperate you are, the more likely you target wrong prospects. Stay in your swimlane.

## Funnel Math (Outbound Sales No Fluff)
Work backwards from quota:
- 25% close rate → need 4 presentations per sale
- 80% meeting hold rate → need 5 scheduled per sale
- 20% meeting rate from conversations → need 25 conversations
- 10% connect rate → need 250 outreach attempts (or 50 prospects x 5 touches)

## Golden Hours (Blount)
- Best cold call times: 8-9am and 4-6pm local time
- Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
- Protect these for selling, not admin
- Never prospect Monday morning or Friday afternoon

## Three Ps Holding You Back (Blount)
1. **Procrastination** — "I'll do it after lunch" → never happens
2. **Perfectionism** — "I need to research more first" → paralysis
3. **Paralysis from Analysis** — overthinking kills action

Cure: block time, start dialing, imperfect action beats perfect inaction.
