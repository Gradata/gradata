# Discovery & Demo Methodology
# Sources: Great Demo! 3rd Ed (Peter Cohan), Doing Discovery (Cohan)
# Auto-load when: demo prep, cheat sheet creation, discovery calls, demo follow-up

## Core Principle: Do the Last Thing First
Show the payoff FIRST. Not features → features → payoff. Show the "Illustration" (the wow output) immediately. Inverted pyramid. Validated by Gong.io data across millions of calls.

## Situation Slide (2 min max, before any product)
Verbalize your understanding. 6 elements:
1. **Job Title & Industry** — who they are
2. **Critical Business Issue (CBI)** — what keeps them up at night
3. **Problems/Reasons** — why the CBI exists
4. **Specific Capabilities** — what they need to solve it
5. **Delta (Value)** — what changes if solved (quantified)
6. **Timeline** — when they need it

Ask: "Did I get that right?" If they correct you, that's BETTER.

## Discovery Skill Levels (Cohan)
- **Level 1:** Uncover pain → leap to solution (novice, insufficient)
- **Level 2:** Uncover pain → ask why (slightly better)
- **Level 3:** Uncover pain → explore impact → broaden across org (understanding impact)
- **Level 4:** Quantify the pain using prospect's own numbers (uncovering value)
- **Level 5:** Reengineer their vision — show possibilities beyond what they imagined
- **Level 6:** Adapt to special cases (burn victims, early adopters, C-suite)
- **Level 7:** Integrated methodology — all skills applied in structured, repeatable process

Target: Level 5+ for every discovery conversation.

## Key Discovery Distinction
- **Qualification** = done for vendor's benefit only (BANT, budget checks)
- **Discovery** = done for BOTH vendor AND prospect's benefit
- If your conversation only benefits you, you're not doing discovery.

## Illustrations, Not Features
- **Feature** = what software does ("We integrate with Meta Ads API")
- **Illustration** = the output that makes them say wow ("Here's your ROAS across all 3 brands — 30 seconds")
- Never demo a feature. Always demo an Illustration.
- Use Before/After framing: painful "before" next to effortless "after"

## Chain of Pain
Pain flows downhill in org charts. Map upward:
- Your contact's CBI → their boss's Problem/Reason → executive's CBI
- The higher you map impact, the higher perceived value and deal size.

## Why Demos Fail
1. No discovery — shows everything, hits nothing
2. Harbor Tour — boring walkthrough of every feature
3. Wrong capabilities shown — breaks trust
4. Monologue — talking AT them for 45 min
5. Save the best for last — they check out before the payoff
6. "Good Little Salesperson" — skip discovery, jump to demo, send proposal, never hear back

## Demo Structure for Sprites (30 min)
1. **Situation Slide** (2 min) — verbalize understanding, ask "did I get that right?"
2. **Discovery deepening** (5 min) — SPIN implication questions before showing anything
3. **Illustration** (3 min) — show the wow output first (unified dashboard, ROAS view)
4. **Specific capabilities** (10 min) — ONLY what solves their stated pain
5. **Pause & question** — every 2-3 min, ask a question before next thread
6. **Close** (5 min) — need-payoff question, then low-risk pilot offer

## Two Demo Types
1. **Technical Proof Demo** — after full discovery. Show specific matches to their CBIs.
2. **Vision Generation Demo** — when discovery is incomplete. Use demo itself as discovery tool. Ask questions BETWEEN demo threads.

Most Sprites demos = Vision Generation. Use the demo to uncover pain, not just prove capability.

## Quick Rules for Cheat Sheets
1. First thing shown = the Illustration (wow output), not the login screen
2. Pause after each thread — ask a question before showing next
3. Quantify every pain into hours/dollars/risk
4. Watch for buying signals — close immediately when they appear
5. If they say something unexpected, follow their pain, don't force the script
6. The prospect is the hero of the story, not your product

## No Decision = Biggest Competitor
45% of B2B opportunities end in "No Decision." This isn't losing to a competitor — it's the prospect choosing to do nothing. Combat with quantified pain and low-risk entry points.
