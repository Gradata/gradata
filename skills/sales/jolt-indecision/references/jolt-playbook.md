## Indecision Pattern Responses

| What They Say | What They Mean | JOLT Response |
|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| "Let me talk to my team" | Afraid of championing something that might fail | Offer to join the team call. Provide a one-pager they can share. "Want me to hop on a quick call with your team? I can answer their questions directly so the context doesn't get lost." |
| "We need to finish [X] first" | Using a real task as excuse to delay a scary decision | "Sprites actually helps with that. Let's connect now so you're ready when [X] is done." |
| "Can I see one more case study?" | Information won't solve this — it's outcome fear | "I can send more, but honestly the best case study is your own account. Let's connect it and see." |
| "What if we try and it doesn't work?" | Pure outcome uncertainty | "$60/month Starter. One account. 30 days. If it doesn't deliver, you're out $60 and gained a free audit." |
| "I need to do more research" | Analysis paralysis | "You've done great diligence. The fastest way to validate from here is a pilot, not more research. Want to start small?" |
| "The timing isn't great" | Could be real or a soft no. Probe. | "Totally get it. When would timing be better? I'll set a reminder and check back." If they can't name a date, it's a soft no. |

## Risk Reversal Framework

The goal is to make saying "yes" feel safer than saying "no." Every risk reversal should answer: "What's the worst that happens if I try?"

**For Sprites specifically:**
1. **Financial risk:** Starter is $60/month. Frame it: "Less than a team lunch. Less than one hour of agency time."
2. **Time risk:** "Connecting your account takes 10 minutes. The audit runs automatically."
3. **Reputation risk (internal champion):** "I'll make sure you look good. Here's a one-pager you can share with your team that shows exactly what we'd do and the expected results."
4. **Switching risk:** "You don't have to cancel anything. Run Sprites alongside your current setup and compare."
5. **Commitment risk:** "Month-to-month. No annual contract on Starter. Cancel anytime."
