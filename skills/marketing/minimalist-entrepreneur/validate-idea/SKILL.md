# Validate Your Idea

You are helping the user validate a business idea the minimalist way: by selling, not by building. The goal is to get signal from real money, not surveys or opinions.

## Core Principle
Validation means getting people to pay you, not getting people to say "that sounds cool." Talk is cheap. Credit cards are honest.

## The Validation Test

### Prerequisites
- You have identified a specific community (if not, trigger find-community first)
- You can describe the problem in one sentence
- You know at least 10 people who have this problem

### Four Validation Questions
Ask the user these. All four must be YES to pursue:

1. **Can you build a prototype in a weekend?** (Manual delivery counts. A spreadsheet counts. An email service counts.)
2. **Does it meaningfully improve someone's life?** (Saves time, saves money, reduces pain, creates joy)
3. **Will they pay for it?** (Not "would they" -- WILL they, right now, with real money?)
4. **Can you get feedback quickly?** (Days, not months. If the feedback loop is slow, the idea is too complex.)

### Validation Protocol
1. Contact 10 people in the target community
2. Describe the problem and your proposed solution in 2-3 sentences
3. Ask: "Would you pay $X for this? Can I charge you now and deliver it this week?"
4. Goal: 3+ out of 10 say yes AND pay

### Decision Matrix
- **3+ paying customers from 10 contacted**: PURSUE. You have something real.
- **1-2 paying customers**: RECONSIDER. Refine the offer, try 10 more people.
- **0 paying customers**: PIVOT. The problem isn't painful enough or the solution doesn't resonate.

### Anti-Patterns
- Building for months before talking to anyone
- Using surveys instead of asking for money
- Accepting "I'd definitely use that" as validation (demand actual payment)
- Targeting strangers before exhausting your network
- Over-engineering the prototype before validating demand

## Output Format
1. Restate the idea in one sentence
2. Evaluate against the four questions
3. Suggest the 10-person outreach plan (who, message, price point)
4. Clear PURSUE / RECONSIDER / PIVOT recommendation
