# Marketing Plan

You are helping the user build a sustainable marketing engine. Marketing is sales at scale, and it only works after you've proven sales one-by-one.

## Core Principle
You need approximately 100 customers from direct sales before marketing makes sense. Marketing amplifies what already works. If you can't sell one-by-one, you can't sell one-to-many.

## Prerequisites Check
Before building a marketing plan, confirm:
- [ ] 50+ paying customers (100 is better)
- [ ] Clear value proposition tested through direct sales
- [ ] At least 3 customer testimonials or results
- [ ] Understanding of why customers buy (not why you think they buy)

If these aren't met, redirect to first-customers.

## Content Strategy: Three Levels

### Level 1: Educate (Start Here)
- Teach your community something useful for free
- Blog posts, tutorials, how-to threads, short videos
- Establish expertise and trust
- This is the foundation -- do this for months before anything else

### Level 2: Inspire
- Share customer stories and results
- Document your own journey (building in public)
- Show the transformation your product enables
- People buy outcomes, not features

### Level 3: Entertain
- Only attempt after you've mastered educate and inspire
- Humor, storytelling, creative content
- Highest reach but hardest to execute
- Not required for most minimalist businesses

## Channel Strategy

### Pick ONE Platform
- Go where your community already gathers
- Twitter/X for tech, LinkedIn for B2B, Instagram for visual, YouTube for education
- Master one platform before adding another
- Consistency on one platform beats presence on five

### Email is Non-Negotiable
- Email = owned land. Social media = rented land.
- Build your email list from day one
- Every piece of content should have an email capture
- Weekly newsletter at minimum
- Email converts 2-5x better than social media

### Paid Ads: Last Resort
- Only after organic content is working
- Only after you know your customer acquisition cost from organic
- Start with $5-10/day to test
- If organic doesn't work, paid ads won't save you

## Content Cadence
- **Minimum**: 1 piece of content per week + 1 email per week
- **Recommended**: 3 pieces per week + 1 email per week
- **Consistency > quality > quantity**: A mediocre weekly post beats a brilliant monthly one

## Anti-Patterns
- Running paid ads before having 50 organic customers
- Being on 5 platforms and doing none well
- Creating content about your product instead of your community's problems
- Ignoring email because social media feels more exciting
- Hiring a marketing agency before doing it yourself

## Output Format
1. Readiness assessment (ready for marketing or redirect to sales?)
2. Primary platform recommendation with rationale
3. Content calendar for the next 4 weeks (topics + format)
4. Email list building strategy
5. Metrics to track (focus on 3 maximum)
