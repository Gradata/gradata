# The JOLT Effect: Overcoming Indecision

40-60% of qualified pipeline is lost to "no decision." Not to competitors, not to status quo — to the customer's fear of making the wrong choice. This is the biggest invisible killer of Sprites deals, and most sales training makes it worse by telling you to push harder.

## Why Traditional Closing Fails Here

The instinct when a deal stalls is to increase urgency — "the price goes up next month," "you're losing money every day you wait." This works when the customer doesn't want to change (status quo preference). But it backfires catastrophically with indecisive buyers, because more pressure creates more anxiety, which creates more paralysis.

**The critical distinction:**
- **Status quo preference** = "I don't want to change." → Increase the pain of inaction.
- **Indecision** = "I want to change but I'm afraid of choosing wrong." → Make it safe to say yes.

If Oliver had a great demo, the prospect was engaged, asked questions, seemed excited — and then went silent — that's almost always indecision, not rejection.

## The Three Sources of Indecision

### 1. Valuation Problems — "Am I choosing the right option?"
Too many choices, too much information. The prospect can't figure out which path is best.

**Signs:** Asking for more comparisons, wanting "one more demo," endless feature questions, researching alternatives indefinitely.

### 2. Lack of Information — "Do I know enough to decide?"
The prospect feels they haven't done enough due diligence.

**Signs:** Requesting more case studies, bringing in new stakeholders late, asking "what else should I be considering?", repeatedly moving the decision date.

### 3. Outcome Uncertainty — "What if it doesn't work for us?"
The prospect fears the purchase won't deliver for THEIR specific situation.

**Signs:** "Our situation is unique," asking for guarantees, wanting the smallest possible commitment, worrying about implementation.

## The JOLT Framework

See [references/jolt-playbook.md](references/jolt-playbook.md) for detailed tactics, email templates, and Sprites-specific deal recovery sequences.

### J — Judge the Level of Indecision
Before solving it, diagnose it. Not every stalled deal is indecision — some are polite rejection.

**Indecision signals:** "I need to think about it," "Can you send more info?", "Let me talk to my team," engaged but anxious.
**Status quo signals:** "We're happy with what we have," "This isn't a priority," disengaged or dismissive.

### O — Offer Your Recommendation
Stop giving options. Start giving recommendations. Indecisive buyers don't want more choice — they want someone they trust to tell them what to do.

"Based on what you told me about [their situation], here's what I'd recommend: start with [specific tier] on [specific account]. Here's why..."

For Sprites: Don't present Starter vs Standard vs Enterprise and ask them to choose. Say: "For a team your size running Meta across 3 brands, Standard is the right fit. And you can start with one brand to validate before rolling out."

### L — Limit the Exploration
Set a boundary on the research process. More information rarely helps — it usually makes paralysis worse.

"You've done thorough due diligence. At this point, another demo won't tell you anything new. The real question is whether the results we showed you are worth testing."

### T — Take Risk Off the Table
Make it psychologically safe to say yes. This is the most powerful lever.

- **Start small:** "Let's start with one brand, prove it works, then roll out."
- **Cost framing:** "$60/month Starter is less than one hour of agency time."
- **Phased approach:** "Month 1: connect and audit. Month 2: launch first campaign. You decide at each step."
- **Specific proof:** "[Company in their exact position] had the same hesitation. Here's what happened."

## How to Apply This Skill

When Oliver has a stalled deal:
1. Diagnose: Is this indecision or a polite no? Look at the signals above.
2. If indecision, identify which source (valuation, information, outcome uncertainty).
3. Draft the right follow-up — recommendation email, risk reversal, or "limit the exploration" message.
4. Help craft the specific language based on what the prospect said in the demo.
5. If the deal has been dark for 7+ days, use the recovery sequence in references.
