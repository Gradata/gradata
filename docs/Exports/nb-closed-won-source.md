# Closed Won Patterns — What Made Deals Convert
## Extracted from Sprites.ai sales calls (March 2026)

### 27 August Apparel — CLOSED WON (Onboarding 3/18)
**Profile:** Multi-brand ecom, $300K/mo ad spend, Shopify Plus, LA-based
**What tipped them:**
- Audit-first approach — showed specific technical issues (Google Merchant Center disconnects, attribution gaps)
- H.M. Cole case study resonated — similar multi-brand structure, went from sub-4 to 5.8 ROAS
- Pilot framing — 4-6 weeks, one brand, minimal commitment
- Specific ROAS improvement target (4 → 5)
- Agency cost comparison — $200K/yr agency vs Sprites
- Justin had direct access to all credentials — fast onboarding
**Buying signals during call:**
- "What about expanding to Amazon and TikTok?" — already thinking beyond pilot
- "Can you integrate with Klaviyo?" — wants to go deeper
- Agreed to onboarding on the spot — scheduled for Wednesday
**Key lesson:** Lead with the audit. Show them what's broken before pitching the fix. Technical credibility > feature list.

### Hassan Ali — LIKELY TO CLOSE (Follow-up pending)
**Profile:** Creative consultant, solo operator, B2B financial services niche
**What moved him:**
- Force multiplier framing — "you do the creative, AI does the media buying"
- Unlimited credits during onboarding — removed experimentation risk
- Acknowledgment that he's "not typical ICP" made him feel understood, not sold to

**Buying signals during call:**

- Explored scaling scenarios — "what if I grow to multiple clients?"
- Expressed desire to "grow alongside the platform"
**Key lesson:** Don't disqualify non-standard ICPs. Creative specialists who want to add media buying are a viable segment. Frame Sprites as their media buying department.

## Conversion Pattern Summary

### What closes deals:
1. **Audit-first approach** — show them what's broken before pitching
2. **Specific numbers** — 5.8x ROAS, 240% organic, $15K→$1K cost reduction
3. **Low-commitment entry** — pilot, trial, one brand, limited credits
4. **Relevant case study** — must match their profile (multi-brand ↔ multi-brand, solo ↔ solo)
5. **Direct credential access** — deals close faster when prospect controls their own accounts

### What doesn't close deals:
1. Generic feature dumps without connecting to their pain
2. Pushing for commitment when they say "not the right time"
3. Overwhelming with pricing tiers before showing value
4. Ignoring the "I need to talk to my boss" signal
